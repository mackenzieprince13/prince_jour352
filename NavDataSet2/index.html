Complaints Against Student Loan Service Compared to U.S. Region Wealth

BY MACKENZIE PRINCE

Navient Data Set Comparison
Infogram
While reviewing the data provided by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), I narrowed my search, finding all complaints made through the CFPB against the Navient Corporation from December 2011 to December 2025. Evidently, due to the 2017 lawsuit against the financial loan service, Navient Corporation recieved the most pushback regarding student loans, peaking at 35,729 complaints filed. Other categories included debt collection, credit reporting and additional personal consumer reports. After searching through the U.S. overall statistics, I broke the information down into states and seperated the results into regions, as seen in my graph. Ultimately, my results showed how region wealth can reflect into who takes out state loans.
According to a 2020 study from the U.S. Department of Agriculture, the country's poverty is disproportionately focused in the southern region with an average poverty rate of over 20%. The findings show how poverty is commonly linked to regions with large rural areas, like the South. Urban communities are believed to create more traffic and generate more revenue, leading to an increase in population percentage and decrease of those in poverty. Due to those below the poverty line in the South, one can assume that southern students may need to invest in student loans to support their education. Therefore, this can explain the graph's large margin between the South and other regions shown.
